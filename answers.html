Pricing

How much does a small-business website cost?

In 2026, a professionally built small-business website typically costs $1,500–$8,000 from an independent studio, with most strategy-led builds landing between $4,000 and $6,000. DIY site builders run $200–$600 a year; larger agencies charge $6,000–$35,000 and up.

Why the huge range? Three things move the number. Strategy: a template shop skips discovery and structure — you're paying for pixels. A strategy-led build starts with your customers and how they decide, which is what makes a site produce enquiries instead of just existing. Scope: page count, e-commerce, booking, and multiple languages all add real work. Visibility: a site that's technically set up to rank on Google and be cited by AI tools takes deliberate engineering that cheap builds skip.

Ongoing costs matter too: hosting (free on modern static builds), a domain (~$15/year), and — the piece most owners discover late — staying visible, which runs $150–$1,500/month depending on how actively you want to grow. AI visibility

How do I show up in ChatGPT and AI search?

AI tools cite pages that answer questions directly: clear question-based headings, a complete answer in the first paragraph, specific facts and quotes, and a site that AI crawlers can technically read. Ranking on Google helps — but it's no longer enough on its own.

Here's the shift: when someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "best hospice in Anaheim" or "affordable web designer near me," the AI writes one answer and names a handful of sources. Industry research in 2026 found the overlap between top Google results and AI-cited pages has fallen below 20% — they're different games with different rules.

What actually earns citations: answer-first writing (the first 200 words answer the question — AI lifts clean, complete passages); question-based headings that match how people really ask; specifics over slogans — research shows quotations, statistics, and cited sources measurably increase AI citations; technical readability — many sites accidentally block AI crawlers or hide content behind scripts; and freshness, because AI answers favor recently updated content and citations fade within months. That last one is why visibility is monthly work, not a one-time setup — it's exactly what our Visibility plan does.

Plain English

SEO vs AEO vs GEO — what's the difference?

SEO gets you ranked in Google's list of links. AEO gets you picked as the direct answer — the box at the top, the voice-assistant reply. GEO gets your business cited when an AI tool like ChatGPT or Gemini writes an answer. Three acronyms, one goal: being the answer your customer finds first.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the established game: earning a spot in the top ten blue links through relevant content, good structure, speed, and credibility. It still matters — a page that ranks well is more likely to be cited everywhere else — but it's the foundation now, not the finish line.

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) targets the direct answer: featured snippets, "people also ask," voice replies. You win it by answering real questions completely and concisely, right at the top of the page. In practice AEO has largely merged into GEO — the techniques overlap.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the new game: when AI writes an answer, it names two to seven sources. GEO is the craft of being one of them — through answer-first content, verifiable specifics, consistent business information across the web, and a site AI can actually read. The door is narrower than ten blue links, which is precisely why the businesses that get this right early own an outsized advantage.

A useful way to hold it: SEO gets you listed, AEO gets you featured, GEO gets you quoted. A modern small-business site should be built for all three at once — that's how we build every site, and what the monthly visibility work keeps sharp.
